About Mallorca

Mallorca, also spelled Majorca, is the largest island in the Balearic Islands, an autonomous community of Spain located in the Mediterranean Sea. The island is famous for its breathtaking coastline, picturesque villages, and vibrant culture. With a history that stretches back thousands of years, Mallorca offers visitors ancient castles, charming markets, scenic mountain ranges, and beautiful beaches. Its capital, Palma, features impressive architecture such as the Gothic Cathedral of Santa Maria. Whether you're interested in hiking, gastronomy, history, or relaxation, Mallorca has something for everyone.
